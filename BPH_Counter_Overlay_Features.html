Overview What is this tool?
01
Runs in the background
A small transparent overlay sits in the top-right corner of the screen. It never blocks work and requires no window switching.
02
Counts bills in real time
Every time a bill is processed, the employee presses Alt + S. The counter goes up instantly on screen.
03
Tracks against hourly goal
Each hour has a target. The color of the overlay tells the employee at a glance whether they are on track or falling behind.
04
Generates a shift report
At any point, pressing F11 shows a complete summary: average rate, total bills, buffer, best hour, and breakdown. After viewing, press F11 again or click the report to close it — the overlay resets to zero and keeps running for the next shift.
05
Remembers your progress
If the app is closed and reopened during the same day, all counts and the accumulated buffer are fully restored.
06
Handles shift exceptions
Break hours (4pm, 6pm) have a reduced goal of 24. The 7pm lunch hour is completely excluded — it neither adds nor removes from the score.
07
Smart overtime detection
Hours worked outside the official shift are tracked as OT. If you open the tool early but don't register any bills, that hour is silently discarded. If you do work, it counts fully — affecting AVG, buffer, and appearing tagged in the report.
08
Auto-reset at 4am
If the tool is left open overnight — PC in sleep mode, forgot to press F11 — the counter resets automatically at 4am, so the next shift always starts clean with no leftover data.
Shift time How the 9-hour shift is counted
Total calendar time: 9 hours (2pm to 11pm)
Effective productive time: 7.5 hours — the two break hours (4pm and 6pm) each contribute only 45 active minutes instead of 60, removing 2 × 15 = 30 minutes from the total.
The 7pm lunch hour contributes 0 minutes — anything done there is not counted.

Theoretical max at 32/hr → 7.5 × 32 = 240 bills  |  At 35/hr → 7.5 × 35 = 262 bills

Section 3B Overtime hours — outside the official shift
Any hour worked before 2pm or after 11pm is treated as overtime. The tool detects it automatically — no setup required. If you open the program early and register nothing, that hour is discarded silently. If you register at least 1 bill, the hour counts fully: same goal of 32, same color thresholds, affects buffer and AVG just like any other hour. The report marks it with OT so you can distinguish it from your regular shift.

Section 4 Accumulated buffer — the running score for the day
The buffer is the running total of how many bills you are ahead of or behind the cumulative goal across all productive hours. It is updated automatically at the end of every hour. A positive buffer means you have done more than required; a negative buffer means you have a deficit to recover.

Section 5 End-of-shift report (F11) — all possible scenarios
Pressing F11 at any point generates the report. It automatically detects what fraction of the current hour is complete, applies the appropriate time weight, and calculates the final average and buffer. The overlay keeps running while you view the report. Closing the report — by clicking it or pressing F11 again — resets everything to zero so the next shift starts clean.

Controls Keyboard shortcuts
Alt + S
Add 1 bill to the current hour count. A 10-second timestamp lock prevents the same press from registering twice — if the system lags or an error occurs, the second trigger within 10 seconds is ignored automatically.
F12
Remove 1 bill (undo last count). Cannot go below 0.
F11
Shows a confirmation dialog — press Yes to show the report (overlay keeps running behind it), No to cancel. Once the report is visible, press F11 again or click anywhere on it to close and reset the counter to zero.
Safeguards Built-in protections against errors
⏱ Alt + S — 10-second lock
Prevents duplicate bill counts
Every time Alt + S is pressed, the tool records a timestamp. If the same key fires again within 10 seconds — due to a system lag, a processing error, or an accidental double-press — the second trigger is silently ignored. The counter only goes up once per valid action.
Press Alt+S → count: 24 → timestamp saved
Error retry within 10s → ignored ✓
Press Alt+S after 10s → count: 25 → OK
⚠ F11 — Report & reset flow
View report, then reset — overlay always keeps running
Pressing F11 shows a confirmation dialog. Confirming opens the report while the overlay continues running behind it. The report stays visible as long as you need. Closing it — by clicking anywhere on it or pressing F11 again — resets the counter, buffer, and log to zero. The overlay is immediately ready for the next shift.
F11 pressed → confirmation dialog
Yes → report shown, overlay keeps running
F11 or click on report → reset to 0, ready for next shift
No in dialog → cancelled, overlay unchanged
🔄 4am auto-reset
Overnight protection — counter resets at 4am
If the program is left open overnight — PC in sleep mode, forgot to press F11 — the counter and all history reset automatically at 4am. The next shift always starts with a clean slate. If you worked OT until 3am, press F11 before 4am to capture the full report before the reset fires.
Forgot F11, PC in sleep → program still open
4:00am arrives → counter resets to 0
Next shift opens clean → no leftover data ✓
⚡ OT zero-count discard
Opened the tool early but didn't work — zero penalty
If you open the overlay before your shift starts and don't press Alt+S at all, the pre-shift hour is silently discarded when the next hour begins. No penalty, no entry in the report, no impact on the buffer. The same applies to any post-shift hour where you registered nothing.
Open at 1:55pm, count = 0
2pm arrives → 1pm hour discarded ✓
Shift starts clean at 2pm → no impact ✓
